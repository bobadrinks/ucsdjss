What is the Jacobs Scholarship?

Recipients of the Jacobs School Scholarship are selected for their academic achievement, demonstrated leadership, commitment to community, and innovative potential. As a community, we work to develop our professional networks, achieve meaningful goals in the field of engineering, and give back to UCSD.



What does it mean to be a Jacobs Scholar?

As a Jacobs Scholar you will have access to a unique network of like-minded scholars, professionals, and industry leaders to give you the connections you need to succeed while studying at one of the top schools in the country.



How do I apply to be a Scholar?

By applying to UCSD, you will automatically be considered for the Jacobs Scholarship. If you receive the scholarship, you will be notified at about the same time you receive your acceptance package.

Events

As members of this scholars society, we have unique opportunities to participate in annual cultural, professional, and social events.


Professional

During Fall quarter, we host our annual Lunch with Professors event. Our own scholars invite professors they are interested in getting to know better. This event provides a great opportunity for our scholars to meet and have meaningful conversations with professors with whom they will be interacting in class and in lab.


Social

At the beginning of each year, we host a Meet-the-Scholars social for the incoming freshman class. The activities vary from year to year, but our Big-Little Family pairings are traditionally revealed at this event. We often coordinate food outings and other activities together. Our small community allows us to really get to know each other!


Cultural

Each year, Mrs. Joan Jacobs hosts a cultural event to meet the Jacobs Scholars and Fellows, and to share her love of the arts with us. This event gives us a great opportunity to immerse ourselves in the arts and to personally say thank you to Dr. and Mrs. Jacobs.

  • 2015: Arms and the Man
  • 2014: Jacobs' Masterworks at the San Diego Symphony
  • 2013: Yoshimi Battles the Pink Robots
  • 2012: Tchaikovsky's Romeo and Juliet
  • 2011: Pines of Rome, A Jacobs' Masterworks Concert
  • 2010: Bonnie & Clyde

How many people get the Jacobs Scholarship every year?

Of the almost 75,000 that apply every year, about 40 students are chosen to receive the Jacobs Scholarship. At UCSD, you will be one of about 40 Scholars campus wide.

How do I make sure I keep the scholarship?

Scholars are expected to maintain a 3.5 GPA to keep the scholarship.

Is this only for Engineering majors? What if I want to switch my major?

In order to hold the scholarship, students must be affiliated with a major in the Jacobs School of Engineering. However, students are able to switch freely between majors within the school regardless of impacted status.

What other benefits do I get as a Jacobs Scholar?

Scholars receive priority enrollment as well as automatic acceptance into their first choice college and major.

Should I come out to the reception?

Definitely! Getting a chance to meet fellow scholars, faculty, and professionals is a great way to get a better sense of what the scholarship is all about and how you'll fit in at UCSD.
